Subgenres & Recommendations

High Fantasy

High fantasy, also known as epic fantasy, is a subgenre of fantasy literature that transports readers to entirely fictional worlds with complex histories, unique cultures, and intricate magic systems. It often features grand, sweeping narratives filled with heroes, villains, and epic quests to save the world. High fantasy worlds are meticulously crafted, and the stories within them explore timeless themes of good vs. evil, heroism, and the struggle for power. These tales are a departure from our reality, offering readers a chance to escape into realms of pure imagination.

These books showcase the rich storytelling, world-building, and character development that define the high fantasy subgenre:

The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien

A cornerstone of high fantasy, this trilogy follows the journey of Frodo Baggins to destroy the One Ring and save Middle-earth from the Dark Lord Sauron.

A Song of Ice and Fire series by George R.R. Martin

Beginning with "A Game of Thrones," this series is known for its sprawling cast of characters and political intrigue in the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros.

The Wheel of Time series by Robert Jordan

Starting with "The Eye of the World," this series spans 14 books and is an epic tale of a world teetering on the brink of an apocalyptic conflict.

Mistborn series by Brandon Sanderson

Beginning with "Mistborn: The Final Empire," this series features a unique magic system based on the consumption of metals and a young heroine's quest to overthrow a dark ruler.

The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss

The first book in the "Kingkiller Chronicle" series, it follows the life and adventures of Kvothe, a gifted musician, and wizard, as he recounts his story to a chronicler.

The Lies of Locke Lamora by Scott Lynch

The first book in the "Gentleman Bastard" series introduces Locke Lamora, a witty thief and con artist in the elaborate city of Camorr.

Low Fantasy

Low fantasy is a subgenre of fantasy literature characterised by its connection to the real world or a world that closely resembles our own. Unlike high fantasy, which typically features entirely fictional settings, low fantasy often introduces fantastical elements into a familiar, everyday environment. In low fantasy, the magical and supernatural exist subtly, and the focus is often on how ordinary people interact with or are affected by these elements. This subgenre blurs the lines between reality and fantasy, creating a sense of wonder in the ordinary.

These titles showcase the diversity of stories that can be told within the low fantasy subgenre, where magic and reality coexist in intriguing ways:

Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling

Set in a magical world hidden within contemporary Britain, this series follows the young wizard Harry Potter as he navigates the challenges of both the wizarding and Muggle (non-magical) worlds.

Neverwhere by Neil Gaiman

In this novel, an ordinary man named Richard Mayhew stumbles into an alternate London, called London Below, where magic, danger, and strange creatures lurk beneath the city's surface.

The Dresden Files series by Jim Butcher

This urban fantasy series follows Harry Dresden, a modern-day wizard and private investigator, as he takes on supernatural cases in Chicago.

Rivers of London series by Ben Aaronovitch

Beginning with "Rivers of London" (also known as "Midnight Riot" in the U.S.), this series features Peter Grant, a London police officer who becomes an apprentice wizard and investigates supernatural crimes.

The Magicians by Lev Grossman

This novel and its sequels introduce readers to Brakebills College for Magical Pedagogy, where students learn magic in a world that closely mirrors our own.

The Golem and the Jinni by Helene Wecker

Set in New York City at the turn of the 20th century, this novel explores the lives of a golem and a jinni who find themselves navigating the human world.
