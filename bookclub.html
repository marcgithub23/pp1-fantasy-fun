Book Club Rules:

Respectful Discussions: Treat fellow members with respect, even when disagreeing. Engage in thoughtful and constructive discussions about the books without personal attacks.

Stay on Topic: Keep discussions focused on the book and the fantasy genre. While some tangential conversation is natural, avoid going too far off-topic.

Spoiler Etiquette: Be mindful of spoilers. Clearly label discussions with spoiler warnings, and avoid revealing major plot twists without warning for those who haven't finished the book.

Participation: Contribute actively to discussions. Share your thoughts, questions, and insights about the book to foster engaging conversations.

Open-Mindedness: Embrace diverse perspectives. Fantasy is a vast genre with various subgenres and themes; be open to exploring different styles and ideas.

Meeting Commitments: Make an effort to attend regularly and be punctual. Respect the agreed-upon meeting times and locations.

Criticise Constructively: While it's okay to critique aspects of a book, do so constructively. Explain why you liked or disliked certain elements, and remember that others may have different tastes.

Maintain a Welcoming Environment: Ensure that the book club remains a welcoming and inclusive space for all members, regardless of their backgrounds or opinions. Discriminatory or offensive language or behaviour is not tolerated.

Rotate Book Selection: Share the responsibility of selecting books among members. This ensures a variety of reading experiences and prevents one member from dominating the choices.

Have Fun: Ultimately, the goal is to enjoy reading and discussing fantasy books. Keep a positive and enthusiastic attitude, and encourage a sense of camaraderie among members. These rules should help create a respectful and enjoyable environment for your fantasy book club. Adjust them as needed to fit the specific dynamics and goals of your group.
